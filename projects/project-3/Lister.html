Project Type Individual
Duration Dec 2018 - Jan 2019
Project Brief
This was a personal-project to design a to-do app which fits the needs of the modern world. Lister is a to-do app that lets you add new tasks according to the priority.
The app can help the user in completing all the tasks without getting unnoticed and regretting later about it.
Research
First of all, I needed to know about the existing to-do apps and how/why a user use that app in a general manner. For this, I created a survey that included both qualitative and quantitative questions. The questions set included queries like:
• What is your age?
• Do you use a to-do app?
• How many tasks do you generally save?
• Were you able to complete all the tasks?
• What is the reason that you cannot complete the task even after saving?

The responses that I received were like:
"Less Time".
"Forgot to open the app and check".
"Conditions changed since I decided to do the task and it became less
important or I did not get enough time to complete it".
User Persona
Primary
Amber, a 19 year old engineering student who make a list of all the tasks she has to do/ had in mind for 1 week. She sometimes forgot to complete the tasks and get tasks mixed for most of the time.
Secondary
James is a 30 year old employee at an IT company who uses a generic to-do app to save the tasks he has to complete for the day plus also saves the daily household tasks. He uses a to-do app atleast 5-6 times a day.
Pain Points
• Don't have enough time.
• Do not remember tasks.
• Tasks became less important.
• Outdated UI.
Solution Thinking
Initially I tried to go with the push-notification for every task a person saves. But after reading about the scope of this solution and analysing some stats it was came to know that almost 50% of app users find push notifications annoying but this also meant that 50% find them useful. So, there was a 50-50 chance whether the user will look at that reminder. I put that idea on hold. Later on, after looking at the problem from a fresh perspective I realised the concept of priority can be used to solve the problem of remembering. To put in clear words a priority (High, Medium, Low) can be assigned for a task which the user think is relevant at that time. Then the tasks can be sorted based on these priority. I find that solution intriguing and started refining this solution.
Result
The final solution was to prioritise the tasks, which than be stacked according to that priority but an option to sort according to the date was also provided to the user. (This solution had some shortcomings as well which I have discussed in the end). After finalising the solution I jumped into designing mid-fidelity wireframes.
Mid Fidelity Wireframes
The wireframes took some time because of the confusion between the position of buttons, which then eventually concluded after some UI articles.
Final Design
I tried to blend the blue color of the background with the transparent/white look of the tasks tiles. The themes section also contain the colors which will complement the background.
Takeaways
Overall, I enjoyed the process and also learnt quite a bit about the science behind a to-do app.
